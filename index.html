Chemical Solution Preparation Tool

Comprehensive tools for standard laboratory preparations (Molar & Normal)
Use when: You need to prepare a solution of known Molarity (M) or Normality (N) by dissolving a pure solid powder/crystal (like NaOH or primary standards like KHP) into a precise volume of water. It calculates the necessary mass (in grams) you need to weigh on a balance.
Use when: You are opening a fresh bottle of a concentrated liquid reagent (like 37% HCl or 98% H₂SO₄) and need to prepare a diluted solution (e.g. 0.1 M) from it. The tool automatically analyzes the bottle's Density and percentage to find the exact Stock Molarity, then tells you how many milliliters to pipette.
Stock Concentration: 0.00 M
Use when: You already have a prepared Stock Solution (e.g., 2 M NaOH) and need to dilute it to a weaker target concentration (e.g., 0.1 M). This applies the universal M₁V₁ = M₂V₂ (or N₁V₁ = N₂V₂) formula.
Dilutes any solution formula (M₁V₁ = M₂V₂ or N₁V₁ = N₂V₂). Using M or N behaves identically.
Use when: You've already weighed a random/specific amount of solid and dissolved it into a final volume, and you need to reverse-calculate what the resulting Molarity (and Normality) is.
Find Molarity (and Normality) backwards when dissolving a known mass in a final volume.
Use when: You need percentage-based solutions rather than Molarity.
• % w/v: Weight/Volume. Used when dissolving solids (e.g., 5% NaCl means 5g of NaCl per 100mL of total solution).
• % v/v: Volume/Volume. Used when mixing two liquids (e.g., 70% Ethanol means 70mL of Ethanol diluted to a total volume of 100mL).
Use when: Preparing extreme trace-level concentrations often used in analytical machines (like AAS, ICP).
• ppm (parts per million) is equivalent to 1 mg/L.
• ppb (parts per billion) is equivalent to 1 μg/L.
Note: Directly preparing ppb from a solid is often impossible due to balance limits; you may be advised to prepare a 1000 ppm stock and serially dilute it.
